Designing molecules with machine learning to power the next generation of semiconductors.

I am a PhD student in Energy Science and Engineering at DGIST, where I develop machine learning and high-throughput computational approaches for the rational design and synthesis of N-type redox-active molecules for semiconducting devices.

My work sits at the intersection of materials chemistry, electrochemistry, and data science. During my MSc at Myongji University, I developed electroactive composite materials for the selective electrochemical recovery of critical metals — lithium, silver, cesium, palladium, and platinum — from industrial and environmental sources.

I enjoy bridging experiment and computation: from stimuli-responsives/semiconductor materials to smart/electronic devices to Python-based ML workflows and density functional theory.

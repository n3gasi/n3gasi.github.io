About

I’m a PhD student focused on AI-assisted materials discovery. My background spans chemical engineering, electrochemistry/batteries, and materials science, and I’m currently building data pipelines and models that connect molecular → film → device properties.

I’m especially interested in developing practical ML systems that work under real lab constraints: limited data, noisy measurements, and multimodal characterization (e.g., spectra, microscopy, scattering).

Current interests

  • GNNs for structure–property prediction (polymers & soft electronics)
  • Surrogate models for device metrics (e.g., mobility/capacitance, transconductance)
  • Data extraction/curation pipelines for literature + lab data
  • Closed-loop optimization (active learning + experiment planning)

Research

Polymer informatics

Represent polymers (repeat units, side chains, distribution) with graph-based and sequence-based models, then learn structure–property relationships at multiple scales.

Organic bioelectronics (OECTs)

Model and optimize organic electrochemical transistors by linking chemistry, microstructure, ion transport, and device performance.

Multimodal data pipelines

Combine molecular descriptors, synthesis/processing variables, and characterization (e.g., GIWAXS, AFM, CV) into ML-ready datasets with clear provenance.
